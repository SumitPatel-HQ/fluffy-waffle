Hackathon 2025 — Global Participant Rules
1. Registration and Eligibility
Teams must register before the official deadline. Late registrations will not be accepted.
A participant may be a member of only one team and one domain.
Once the event begins, team composition cannot be changed.
2. Devices and Development Setup
Participants must bring their own laptops and required development devices.
Organizers are not responsible for providing personal development hardware.
Teams must ensure that all required software, libraries, and tools are installed before the event begins.
3. Project Development Rules
All projects must be developed during the hackathon event period.
Pre-existing projects or previously developed codebases are not allowed, except for publicly available libraries and frameworks.
Teams must ensure their solution is demonstrable during evaluation.
Where applicable, projects should be accessible on both laptop and mobile devices if required by the problem statement.
4. Submission Requirements
Final project code and documentation must be submitted via a GitHub repository or other specified platform depending on the domain.
The repository must include source code, setup instructions, a project description, and a demo link or demonstration material.
Submissions must be made before the official submission deadline. Late submissions will not be evaluated.
5. AI Tools and Prompt Logging
Participants are allowed to use AI tools for development, design, or research.
All significant prompts used with AI tools must be logged during the event.
Failure to log prompts may negatively impact evaluation in AI-related criteria.
6. Event Rounds
The hackathon consists of two main evaluation rounds.
Round 1 – Lab Round: Teams develop their solution and submit their project. Judges evaluate projects based on the domain rubric.
Round 2 – Finals: The top teams from each domain advance to the finals and present their project to a new judging panel for final evaluation.
7. Attendance and Venue Conduct
Participants should remain within the event venue once the event has started unless authorized by the organizers.
Teams must be available for judging and demonstrations when requested.
Participants must maintain professional conduct and respect toward other teams, judges, and organizers.
8. Fair Play and Academic Integrity
All work submitted must be original and created during the event.
Any form of plagiarism, copying, or unauthorized collaboration between teams is strictly prohibited.
If plagiarism or misconduct is detected, the team will be immediately disqualified.
9. Judging and Organizer Authority
Judges’ decisions are final and binding.
Organizers reserve the right to modify schedules, rules, or judging procedures if necessary.
Any disputes must be addressed through the official organizer communication channel.
10. Safety and Responsibility
Participants are responsible for the safety and security of their personal devices and belongings.
Organizers will not be liable for loss or damage of personal property during the event.
11. Disqualification Conditions
A team may be disqualified if they violate event rules, submit plagiarized or pre-built projects, fail to submit before the deadline, harass or sabotage other participants, or attempt to manipulate judging or leaderboards.
